# Keyboard Guide

An on-screen keyboard that floats over the bottom of every lesson and shows the learner which key to press next — and which finger to press it with. It removes the scariest part of typing for beginners: hunting the physical keyboard with your eyes while losing your place on screen.

---

## How to Use It

1. **Start any lesson** — a [typing lesson](typing-lessons.md) or a [web lesson](web-lessons.md). The keyboard appears at the bottom of the screen automatically; it's on by default.
2. **Watch the glowing key.** The key for the next character pulses dark with a green glow. Press it and the glow moves to the next key.
3. **Follow the colors.** Every key is tinted by which finger should press it — eight soft colors, one per finger, with the thumb on the space bar. The label under the keyboard names the finger, e.g. *left index*.
4. **Capitals and symbols** — when the next character needs Shift (like `A` or `<`), the correct Shift key lights up too, always on the *opposite* hand from the character key. That's the habit worth building from day one.
5. **F and J have bumps**, just like a real keyboard — home base for your index fingers.

The guide never reacts to wrong keys. The red cursor flash in the lesson is the only feedback — show and forgive.

---

## Showing and Hiding

- Click the round **keyboard button** in the bottom-right corner to slide the keyboard off screen. Click it again to bring it back.
- The choice is **remembered per learner** — if Maya hides it and Sam doesn't, each sees their own preference next time.
- The guide hides itself on touch-only devices and very narrow windows, where a map of a physical keyboard doesn't help.

---

## Good to Know

- The layout is **US QWERTY**. Learners on other physical layouts (AZERTY, QWERTZ) will see mismatched keys.
- The guide is a hint, not a crutch — encourage learners to glance at it less as their accuracy grows, or hide it for a challenge run.
